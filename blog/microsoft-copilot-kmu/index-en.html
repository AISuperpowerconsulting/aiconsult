Microsoft Copilot promises to revolutionize productivity in Office 365. But at CHF 30 per user per month, SMEs face a crucial question: Do the time savings justify this investment? In this practical guide, we analyze features, ROI, and real-world use cases.

What is Microsoft Copilot?

Microsoft Copilot is an AI assistant integrated directly into Microsoft 365. It uses GPT-4 technology from OpenAI and has access to your company data in:

  • Word: Write, summarize, rewrite texts
  • Excel: Analyze data, create formulas, generate visualizations
  • PowerPoint: Create presentations from documents, design suggestions
  • Outlook: Summarize emails, suggest responses, coordinate appointments
  • Teams: Meeting summaries, extract action items
  • OneNote, Loop, Whiteboard: Structure notes, organize ideas

The key difference: Unlike ChatGPT, Copilot can access your internal documents, emails, and files ‚Äì working context-aware within your company.

Limitations & Disadvantages

Copilot isn't perfect. You should know these limitations:

1. High Entry Barrier

Employees need 2-4 weeks to use Copilot effectively. Training is mandatory.

2. Variable Quality

Outputs must always be reviewed. For complex tasks, Copilot sometimes delivers unusable results.

3. Privacy Concerns

Copilot has access to all M365 data. Sensitive documents could unintentionally flow into prompts.

Conclusion: Is Microsoft Copilot Worth It?

Yes, for most SMEs. The investment of CHF 30/month per user pays for itself after just a few days ‚Äì provided:

  1. You use Microsoft 365 intensively
  2. You invest in training
  3. Your team actively uses the tools (at least 30% of possible features)

The biggest benefits aren't the absolute time savings, but the quality of time gained: Instead of drowning in emails and admin work, your employees can focus on value-creating activities.
