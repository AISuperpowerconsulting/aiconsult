Overview of the New Swiss Data Protection Act (FADP)

The revised FADP brings significant changes to handling personal data:

Key Changes

  • Extended Information Obligations: Companies must transparently inform about data processing
  • Privacy by Design: Data protection must be integrated into processes from the start
  • Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA): Mandatory for high-risk data processing
  • Breach Notification Requirement: Serious violations must be reported to the FDPIC
  • Higher Fines: Up to CHF 250,000 for intentional violations

Important: The FADP applies to all companies in Switzerland, regardless of size. SMEs must also fully comply with all requirements.

ChatGPT and Data Privacy: The Critical Points

1. Data Transfer to the USA

ChatGPT is operated by OpenAI, a US company. This means:

  • Your inputs are processed on servers in the USA
  • US authorities could theoretically access this data (CLOUD Act)
  • Swiss FADP requires special protective measures for foreign data transfers

2. Use of Training Data

OpenAI has adjusted its privacy policies multiple times:

  • Free Version: Inputs may be used for AI training by default (unless disabled)
  • ChatGPT Plus/Enterprise: Data is not used for training
  • API Usage: Business customers can opt out of data usage

3. Lack of Transparency

Challenges for FADP compliance:

  • Not fully transparent how data is processed
  • Uncertainty about retention periods and deletion deadlines
  • Limited access and deletion options for affected persons

Practical Guidelines for SMEs

‚úÖ What You Can Safely Input

  • General professional questions without personal reference
  • Publicly available information
  • Anonymized data (completely without traceability)
  • Sample texts without real customer data
  • Theoretical scenarios and concepts

‚ùå What You Should Never Input

  • Personal Data: Names, addresses, emails, phone numbers of customers/employees
  • Particularly Sensitive Data: Health data, biometric data, political views
  • Business Secrets: Confidential contracts, price calculations, strategy plans
  • Contract Documents: Complete customer contracts, terms with customer reference
  • Internal Communication: Emails, chat histories with identifiable persons

5 Steps to FADP-Compliant ChatGPT Usage

Step 1: Create Data Privacy Guidelines

Document how ChatGPT may be used in your company. Clearly define which data may be entered and which may not.

Step 2: Train Employees

Conduct workshops showing examples of permitted and prohibited inputs. Raise awareness of data privacy risks.

Step 3: Implement Technical Measures

Consider ChatGPT Enterprise or API solutions with privacy guarantees. Implement anonymization tools before input.

Step 4: Update Processing Activity Register

Document ChatGPT usage in your data processing register according to Art. 12 FADP.

Step 5: Regular Reviews

Conduct quarterly audits. Adapt your guidelines to new developments (e.g., changes at OpenAI).

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Can I anonymize customer names and then input them into ChatGPT?

Only if the anonymization is truly complete. Don't just replace names, but also all other identifying features (project names, location names, specific details). When in doubt: better refrain.

Can I use ChatGPT for marketing texts?

Yes, ChatGPT can be used without problems for general marketing texts without customer reference. However, avoid entering specific customer information or targeting data.

Do I need to inform my customers if I use ChatGPT?

If you were to input customer data into ChatGPT (which you shouldn't!), yes. For general use without personal data, no customer notification is required. However, update your privacy policy if you use AI tools for data processing.

Is ChatGPT Plus better from a privacy perspective than the free version?

Yes, with ChatGPT Plus your inputs are not used for AI training by default. However, for companies, ChatGPT Enterprise or an API solution with a data processing agreement is more advisable.

What happens in case of a data protection violation?

Intentional violations of the FADP can result in fines up to CHF 250,000. Additionally, customers may claim damages, and your company's reputation can suffer significantly.

Conclusion: Use ChatGPT Responsibly

ChatGPT is a powerful tool that can significantly increase productivity in SMEs. The key to legally compliant use lies in:

  1. Clear Internal Guidelines: Define exactly what may be entered
  2. Employee Training: Raise awareness of data privacy risks among your team
  3. Technical Safeguards: Use business versions with better guarantees
  4. Regular Checks: Review compliance with your guidelines

With these measures, you can use ChatGPT without violating the FADP ‚Äì and benefit from the enormous productivity gains that AI offers.
