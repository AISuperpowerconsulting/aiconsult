AGI, Animal Welfare, US Politics: What Swiss SMEs Need to Know Now

The world of Artificial Intelligence is racing forward – often in directions we don't expect. While AI policy is being decided at the highest level in the US, researchers are discussing how AGI could revolutionize animal welfare. But what do these seemingly distant developments mean for your Swiss SME? It's time to look beyond the horizon and understand the global waves that are also shaping our local AI landscape.

The Unexpected Front: AGI and Animal Welfare – A Lesson for Every SME

Imagine you're sitting at the breakfast table, reading that Artificial General Intelligence (AGI) has the potential to transform animal welfare – a topic that, at first glance, might seem to have little to do with your SME in Switzerland. The term 'AGI-pilled' describes the conviction that AGI will have a profound impact on animal welfare, whether through the development of tastier and cheaper alternatives to animal products or the use of robots in livestock farming. Also interesting is the discussion on how the values of future AGIs can be influenced to prioritize animal welfare, a concern for leaders in AGI labs like OpenAI, DeepMind, or Anthropic.

What can we learn from this? First: The reach of AI is limitless and surprising. Topics considered niche today can become mainstream tomorrow through AI developments. Second: Ethical questions are inextricably linked to AI. The concern that AGI could make factory farming even more cost-effective, or the observation of a 'speciesist bias in AI' that ignores the impact of AI on non-human animals, show how crucial a comprehensive ethical consideration is. For your SME, this means: Even if you don't use robots in barns, you must address the ethical implications of your AI use. What biases might be hidden in your data or algorithms? How do you ensure your AI systems operate fairly and responsibly?

The 'One Welfare' approach, which integrates animal welfare, human well-being, and environmental protection, envisions AI-powered systems as 'co-pilots.' They are meant to provide data-based recommendations without completely replacing human decisions. This is an excellent model for every Swiss SME: AI should support you, improve your decisions, and increase efficiency, but never replace human expertise and responsibility. It's about intelligently and ethically integrating AI to benefit from its potential while minimizing risks. The challenge lies in selecting valid indicators for well-being (be it animal, human, or business process) and ensuring data quality for ethically and accurately functioning AI systems.

The White House Speaks Plainly: Global AI Policy and Switzerland

While we in Switzerland eagerly await the implementation of the EU AI Act and the adaptation of our Swiss DPA (DSG), the US government is setting clear signals in global AI policy. Between 2025 and 2026, several important executive orders were published, setting the direction. For example, on January 23, 2025, Executive Order 14179, 'Removing Barriers to American Leadership in Artificial Intelligence,' was issued. This was followed by measures such as 'Promoting the Export of the American AI Technology Stack' or 'Accelerating Federal Permitting of Data Center Infrastructure.'

Why is this relevant for your Swiss SME? Simply put: The USA is a global trendsetter. Its policies influence international standards, competition, and the availability of AI technologies. If the US removes barriers to AI leadership and promotes the export of its AI technology, it means more intense global competition. Swiss SMEs operating internationally or collaborating with US partners must keep an eye on these developments. The acceleration of data center infrastructure permits also shows that data processing capacities are being massively expanded – a cost factor and a strategic consideration for any company relying on data-intensive AI applications.

Another interesting point is the Executive Order 'Preventing Woke AI in the Federal Government' from July 23, 2025. Regardless of the political alignment of the term 'Woke AI,' this signals the growing importance of bias, fairness, and neutrality in AI systems. For your SME, this means that the demand for unbiased and transparent AI solutions is becoming louder and louder. Whether in human resources, customer analysis, or product development – you must ensure that your AI applications do not discriminate or reproduce unwanted biases. This is not only an ethical but increasingly also a legal and reputational necessity, which is also underpinned by the EU AI Act. The developments in the USA are therefore an important indicator of global trends that will sooner or later also arrive in Switzerland.

From Global Headlines to Local Strategy: Your SME in the AI Era

The rapid development of AI, be it in animal welfare or US politics, may seem distant at first glance. Yet, it underscores a central message: AI is unstoppable and increasingly influences more areas of our lives and economy. For your Swiss SME, it is crucial to understand this dynamic and act proactively, rather than just reacting. The 'Awesome-LLM-RAG' collection on GitHub (with 1314 stars) shows how quickly advanced techniques like Retrieval Augmented Generation (RAG) are evolving – an area that could also be relevant for the efficiency of your language models. Projects like RAGLite (with 1.1k stars on GitHub) also indicate constant innovation. The pace is high, and those who don't keep up risk falling behind.

What can you do specifically? Start with an inventory: Where are you already using AI? Where could you profitably use it to reduce costs or increase revenue? Think of proven use cases, as highlighted by the trends for 2026: automated customer communication, data-driven decision-making, process optimization. But equally important is the other side of the coin: governance. Robust AI governance is not just a reaction to the EU AI Act or the Swiss DPA (DSG), but a strategic necessity. It helps you minimize risks, build trust, and responsibly leverage the full potential of AI.

Consider how you can create internal guidelines that ensure the ethical use of AI in your company. How do you train your employees in dealing with AI? How do you address data protection, bias, and transparency? Global developments show that these questions can no longer be ignored. They are the cornerstone for successful and sustainable integration of AI into your business. Be proactive, be informed, and actively shape the AI future of your SME, instead of letting it overwhelm you.
